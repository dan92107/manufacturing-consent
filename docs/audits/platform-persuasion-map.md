# 🧠 PLATFORM COMPARISON — DISTORTION DYNAMICS

## ⚡ 1. TikTok

### **Format Pressure**

* ultra-short
* high velocity
* visual + emotional dominance

### **Distortion Patterns**

* **extreme compression**
  → complex ideas → 1–2 claims
* **binary framing**
  → awake vs asleep thrives here
* **emotional spikes**
  → fear + empowerment loops

### **Algorithm Bias**

* rewards:

  * retention
  * replays
  * emotional reaction

→ selects for **intensity over accuracy**

### **Engagement vs Truth**

* engagement = primary currency
* truth = incidental if it performs

**Net Effect:**
→ strongest drift toward **narrative inflation + identity locking**

---

## ▶️ 2. YouTube

### **Format Pressure**

* medium to long form
* narrative continuity
* creator authority building

### **Distortion Patterns**

* **authority stacking**
  → multiple books, theories, disciplines
* **structured storytelling**
  → hook → reveal → conclusion
* **repetition across segments**

### **Algorithm Bias**

* rewards:

  * watch time
  * session duration
  * “next video” chaining

→ selects for **coherent, immersive narratives**

### **Engagement vs Truth**

* partial balance:

  * can include nuance
  * but still favors **compelling over precise**

**Net Effect:**
→ ideal environment for **system-building narratives**
(not just claims, but worldviews)

---

## 📰 3. News (Mainstream)

### **Format Pressure**

* institutional constraints
* time-bound reporting
* credibility signaling

### **Distortion Patterns**

* **compression (headline logic)**
* **omission (space + framing limits)**
* **authority filtering** (approved experts)

### **Algorithm / Structural Bias**

* incentives:

  * advertiser safety
  * institutional alignment
  * reputational risk management

→ selects for **acceptable narratives within bounds**

### **Engagement vs Truth**

* aims for credibility
* but shaped by:

  * selection bias
  * framing bias

**Net Effect:**
→ **narrowed reality**, not total fabrication
(structural filtering, not identity myth-making)
