# Failure Modes

## Purpose

This file documents recurring risks where analysis may drift from structured skepticism into distortion, overreach, or unreliable inference.

## Failure Modes Being Monitored

### 1. Narrative Inflation

Description:

Interpretive language becomes stronger than available evidence.

Example:

“Policy dispute” becomes “proof of authoritarian control.”

Risk:

Framing overtakes fact.

---

### 2. Pattern Over-Detection

Description:

Noise is mistaken for coordinated signal.

Example:

Independent events are read as evidence of hidden design.

Risk:

False coherence.

---

### 3. Incentive Overreach

Description:

Real incentives are assumed to fully explain behavior.

Example:

Financial or political incentives are treated as sufficient proof of intent.

Risk:

Reductionism.

---

### 4. Hidden-Actor Inference

Description:

Analysis implies covert coordination without evidentiary support.

Example:

Unseen actors are inferred from ambiguity alone.

Risk:

Speculation posing as analysis.

---

### 5. Structural Drift into Ideology

Description:

System-level critique hardens into totalizing worldview.

Example:

“All institutions are manipulation systems.”

Risk:

Skepticism becomes doctrine.

---

### 6. Suspicion Presented as Proof

Description:

Questions or suspicions are treated as evidence.

Risk:

Epistemic collapse.

---

## Drift Warning Signs

Watch for:

* “This proves…” language with weak evidence
* Single-cause explanations for complex events
* Omitted alternative explanations
* Hidden coordination assumptions
* Certainty rising as evidence weakens

---

## Correction Protocol

If a failure mode appears:

1. Reclassify fact vs interpretation vs narrative.

2. Generate alternative explanations.

3. Run self-critique:

Where might this be overreaching?

4. Revise analysis under stricter evidence standards.

---

## Design Principle

The system should fail toward uncertainty, not false certainty.
